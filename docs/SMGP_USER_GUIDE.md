# AMS2 Career Companion — SMGP user guide

The Super Monaco GP (SMGP) module: a 17-season fictional-world racing career around real AMS2
races. You climb from the back of the grid to the summit across four eras, against a fixed
34-driver universe led by A. Senna, the permanent benchmark.

## 1. Starting a career

- On the main menu, pick **Super Monaco GP** (PLAYABLE NOW).
- The wizard shows the campaign pack, verifies your installed AMS2 content, and asks for:
  - **Your character**: name, nationality, stats, perks, and a Racing DNA identity (30 authored
    identities with real effects). Creation spends Character Points (CP); per level you earn
    Skill Points (SP) to buy perks or stat steps on the skill tree.
  - **Mortality**: Off / Normal / Hardcore. Off means no injuries or death. Normal adds the full
    save-and-reload safety net. Hardcore has no saves and a fatal accident permanently deletes
    the career file. The choice is fixed at creation and shown on your dossier (MORTALITY label).
- You start at the bottom (a LEVEL D team) and earn everything above through the rival ladder.

## 2. The weekly loop

1. **Briefing** — the circuit, weather, format, your rival's status, your car's spec, the
   checklist, and the season lore header (each of the 17 seasons has its own title and era).
2. **Grid** — the full field in qualifying order. SMGP uses a seeded DNQ field: the backmarkers
   rotate each round (and each season), so who even makes the show varies.
3. **Race in AMS2** — the app stages the grid (custom AI + liveries, always with a timestamped
   backup) and you drive. Launch AMS2 directly, not through a mod manager.
4. **Result entry** — type or drag the classification (qualifying first on weekend formats).
   The fold is deterministic: standings, records, XP, and news all derive from what you enter.
5. **Aftermath** — the progression toast (+XP, level, SP), news and dispatches, your rival's
   response, then the next briefing. After two wins in a season a promotion offer may come —
   accept or stay loyal.

## 3. Progression

- **Levels**: results award XP by expectation, not just position — beating your expected finish
  is the engine. The cap is **LEVEL 300 · MAX**; the dossier shows the badge at the top.
- **Skill Points**: 3 per level, spent on the skill tree (perks across nine branches) or +0.05
  stat steps. The full 499-SP pool unlocks by the season-16 review, so the final season can be
  driven with your complete build.
- **Stats**: pace, one-lap, craft, racecraft, adaptability (talent) + marketability, durability
  (meta). Durability toughens you against the accident ladder; marketability feeds offers.
- **Reset/respec**: respec tokens arrive every 5 levels for equal-or-lower-cost swaps.

## 4. Injuries, sitting out, and death

- Accident DNFs draw a severity (Light/Medium/Heavy) and a deterministic roll: none / a short
  injury / season-ending / death. Light shunts are mostly harmless.
- An injury sits you out: the calendar marks SAT OUT / WILL MISS, the round auto-simulates with
  you DNS, and the medical record on your dossier keeps the whole history.
- Death (Normal/Hardcore only) is permanent: the death screen, the gallery IN MEMORIAM badge,
  and the career archive stays readable. Hardcore deletes the file.
- A Level-D floor knock-out (repeated last-place battles) ends the career with a game-over
  screen, no soft continue.

## 5. News, history, and the record book

- The News tab is the living newsroom: race reports, milestones, rivalries, canon divergence
  when your results beat the world's remembered champions. History keeps everything forever:
  scrapbook cards, the encyclopedia, the campaign timeline strip, and your career timeline.
- Standings, records, and head-to-heads are exact: the points engine is proven against 77 real
  historical seasons.

## 6. Saves and seasons

- Seasons roll into each other at the review (offers, carryover, the rival ladder). Season 17
  ends the campaign at the finale; a flawless run (all 17 titles) unlocks THE EMPEROR RUN.
- Normal mortality: save slots with labels, autosaves at season starts, restore any time.
- The gallery is your collection: every career is a self-contained file with terminal badges for
  finished ones.

## 7. Tips

- Beat your expected finish, not the world: XP flows from overperformance.
- Watch the DNQ strip: qualifying matters for the back of the field too.
- Heavy accidents are the killers; durability is cheap insurance on Normal.
- The debug menu (Ctrl+Shift+F12 to unlock, Ctrl+Shift+D) previews anything without grinding,
  developer-only.
